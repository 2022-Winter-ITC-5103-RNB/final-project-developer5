Maharashtra

Mumbai is the capital of Maharashtra, an Indian state. With a population of almost 2 crore, Mumbai is India's second-most populous metropolis after Delhi and the world's eighth-most populous city, according to the United Nations. According to the 2011 Indian government population census, Mumbai was India's most populated city, with a population of 1.25 crore living in the Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai. With a population of over 2.3 crore, Mumbai is the centre of the Mumbai Metropolitan Region, the world's sixth most populated metropolitan area. Mumbai is located on India's west coast, on the Konkan coast, and boasts a deep natural harbour. Mumbai was the most populous city in India in 2008.

Major Tourist Attractions

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India, which stands proudly on the banks of the Arabian Sea in the Apollo Bunder waterfront district, is one of Mumbai's most popular and revered sights. The 26-meter basalt archway was created as a welcome gesture for King George V and Queen Mary when they visited British India in 1911. It combines the architectural forms of Roman triumphal arches with traditional Hindu and Muslim patterns. Ironically, the last British troops left India via this archway in 1948, when the country achieved independence from the United Kingdom.

The Gateway of India is now one of Mumbai's most popular gathering locations for both tourists and locals. From the five jetties that flank the arch, you can watch yachts and ferries set sail. Street food sellers sell the famed bhelpuri (a crispy, salty delicacy composed of puffed rice and drenched with tamarind sauce). Alternatively, you may simply spend an hour or two people-watching at this bustling attraction.

Bollywood Tour

Since the 1930s, when the Bombay Talkies movie studio was created in the city, Bollywood, India's largest film industry, has had a stronghold in Mumbai. Every year, Bollywood produces over to 1,000 films, ranging from historical epics to curry westerns to courtesan movies.

On a full-day private Bollywood tour, you can get a behind-the-scenes glimpse at this blockbuster machine. Tourists will visit two Bollywood film studios, pose for photos with the stars (subject to scheduling availability), watch Bollywood dancing and learn some choreography, and drive through the luxury neighbourhood where the top Bollywood actors and actresses live on this excursion.

Bandra-Worli Sea Link

The Bandra-Worli Sea Link, a cable-stayed bridge, is one of Mumbai's most iconic sights. The eight-lane bridge, which opened in 2009, connects the central business area with the western suburbs across the open sea, providing much-needed relief from the Mahim Causeway's congestion. It has become Mumbai's counterpart of the Brooklyn Bridge or the Opera House in New York.

Head to the Bandra Fort, a historic watchtower built by the Portuguese in 1640, for the best views of the Bandra-Worli Sea Link. It's also worthwhile to drive across the bridge. The crashing water may be seen on both sides of the bridge during the day. Watch the bridge light up with stunning colours at night.
