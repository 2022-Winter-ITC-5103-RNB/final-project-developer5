Munnar

Munnar is a town and hill station on Western Ghats mountain ranges located in the Idukki district of the southwestern Indian state of Kerala.Munnar is also called the "Kashmir of South India". Munnar is well connected by both National highways, state highways and rural roads. It is Known for its evergreen tea plantations, the hills of Munnar with their rolling beauty and charm can cast a magical spell on the visitors! While most of the hills are adorned with tea plantations, some are enchanted by mesmerising waterfalls, lush vegetation and lot more. Munnar wears a pleasant climate throughout the year. Among all the popular tourist destinations in Kerala, this is one such destinations that is known for the liveliness in its weather and climate. Munnar simply proves to be a paradise for the wildlife lovers! Home to some of the most beautiful and dense wildlife sanctuaries and wildlife protected areas, this gorgeous hill station in Kerala can make you forget the outer world and delve deep into the wilderness!

Silent Valley

Silent vally is a National Park which is in Kerala. It is located in the Nilgiri Hills. It has some rare species of flora and fauna.The national park is one of the last undisturbed tracts of South Western Ghats mountain rain forests and tropical moist evergreen forest in India. The first Western investigation of the watersheds of the Silent Valley area was in 1857 by the botanist Robert Wight.Silent Valley is rectangular, twelve kilometres from north to south and seven from east to west. The Kunthipuzha River drains the entire 15 km length of the park from north to south into the Bharathapuzha River. Kunthipuzha River divides the park into a narrow eastern sector of width 2 kilometers and a wide western sector of 5 kilometers. Birdlife International lists 16 bird species in Silent Valley as threatened or restricted:- Nilgiri wood-pigeon, Malabar parakeet, Malabar grey hornbill, white-bellied treepie, grey-headed bulbul, broad-tailed grassbird, rufous babbler, Wynaad laughing thrush, Nilgiri laughing thrush, Nilgiri blue robin, black-and-rufous flycatcher, Nilgiri flycatcher, white-bellied blue-flycatcher, crimson-backed sunbird and Nilgiri pipit.

Edakkal Caves

Edakkal Caves are two natural caves at a remote location at Edakkal around 25 km from Kalpetta in the Wayanad district of Kerala in India's Western Ghats.The Stone Age carvings of Edakkal are rare and are the only known examples from South India besides those of Shenthurini, Kollam also in Kerala.The caves contain drawings that range over periods from the Neolithic as early as 6,000 BC to 1,000 BCE.The caves contain drawings that range over periods from the Neolithic as early as 6,000 BC to 1,000 BCE. The etchings and prehistoric line drawings are a testament to the culture and lives of those who lived in the caves years ago.The Edakkal cave paintings are remarkable in their sheer scale and complexity, but their origins are shrouded in mystery. Ever since their discovery in 1895, they have been a source of awe and wonder for tourists and historians alike.The engravings are not the only attractions at Edakkal. The Muniyaras, or ancient burial sites that have been discovered in these hills have yielded a rich collection of ancient earthenware and pottery. Most of the artifacts discovered here are now housed in the Wayanad Heritage Museum.
